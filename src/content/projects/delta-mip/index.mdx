**delta-mip** is the codec that fell out of [shrinkray](/lab/projects/shrinkray)'s reversibility research, spun into its own focused project. It encodes the *residual* between a downscaled texture and its original so a single bitstream can serve two masters: **byte-exact lossless restore** when you want the original back, and **lossy distribution** when you only need it to look right.

## Honest numbers

The headline result is deliberately unglamorous: **+14.7% net** over a raw backup on BC7 textures, byte-exact. That's a real, measured win — not a hype figure — and it comes from validation across a corpus, not a cherry-picked sample. An earlier version looked far better at small *n* and collapsed when the sample grew; delta-mip is what survived that honesty.

## Why it exists

Generic compressors don't know they're looking at block-compressed GPU textures. delta-mip is content-aware about exactly that structure, which is where the margin over a plain `zstd` backup comes from. It's source-available (no redistribution) Rust, and the long arc is a graphics-paper-grade story about class-routed reversible compression for game assets.
