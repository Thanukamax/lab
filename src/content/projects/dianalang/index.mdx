**dianalang** is a small domain-specific language for writing voice-assistant skills — the kind [Diana](/lab/projects/diana) runs — where the dangerous parts are caught by the *compiler*, not by hoping the author got it right.

## Safety as a language feature

The premise: a skill that shells out, touches files, or runs privileged actions is a security surface. In a normal scripting setup you defend that with discipline and review. dianalang instead makes it a property of the language — a skill **compiles only if it's injection-proof and its dangerous capabilities are explicitly gated**. If you can't prove it's safe, it doesn't build.

## Why I built it

It came out of a real problem: as Diana's skill catalogue grew past 60 entries, each new skill was another chance to introduce a command-injection or an un-gated privileged action. Pushing that check down into a compile step turns "remember to be careful" into "the build fails." It's experimental and small on purpose — the interesting part is the idea, not the line count.
